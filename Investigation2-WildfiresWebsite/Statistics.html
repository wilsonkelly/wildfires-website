Statistics

Social:

In November of 2018, the Camp Fire burnt through Northern California, becoming the deadliest and most destructive fire in the state's history. According to the California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection, 18,804 residential, commercial, and other structures were destroyed, and 85 people were killed.
California is at rank 1 of states with high to extreme wildfire risk in 2019, with 2,019,800 properties at risk (Source).

Economical:

The Insurance Information Institute estimated the Camp Fire totaled between $8.5 billion and $10.5 billion in dollars when it occurred (Source). AccuWeather predicts 2018 wildfires cost California alone losses of $400 billion, making it the most expensive natural disaster in the history of the US. To put that into perspective, that amount of money is equivalent to four tenths of a trillion dollars, and 2% of the US's GDP. The estimate is made on loss of value in property, but also health impacts (Source).

The image on the right describes wildfire financial damage in 2017-2018, marked in red,compared with other years. Graphed at the top 3 are the Camp, Tubbs, and Woolsey Fire, costing over $25 billion (Source).

Environmental:

Growing climate risk increases the frequency and severity of natural disasters, shifting weather patterns. Climate change and changing weather patterns suggest the wildfire risk will only continue to rise (Source).
